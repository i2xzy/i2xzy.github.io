Past

I was born in Hackney and have lived in and around the borough most of my life. Going to a religious school for me was frustrating as all the lessons I really enjoyed were allocated only 90 minutes at the end of the day. When I was in Year 8 they got rid of secular studies entirely but I wouldn’t take it and I was allowed to join Year 7 while the rest of my peers went home.

This was the beginning of me becoming more skeptical and learning on my own initiative. In the next few years, while I was failing at my religious studies, I learned by myself the Cyrillic alphabet, basic Japanese, Adobe Illustrator, Sketchup and also, HTML and CSS. I knew then already that I would get into coding…

All the jobs I’ve had to date included some form of computer skills. Even if it was not in the original job description. Servicing Apple computers, e-commerce, graphic design and even in the school I worked for I became the default IT-guy.

This also led me even more toward programming as I started helping the teachers with the, then new, Computing curriculum. I learned how to use the Beebot and Probot as well as MIT’s Scratch.

For multiple reasons I moved away from that line of work...

Present

I currently work for an office in the London diamond district as an office assistant and gem sorter. The only interesting computer work I get to do is occasionally messing around with Excel functions. Figuring out how to effectively use them still give me actual joy.

The job is quite time-intensive but I do still have those light-bulb moments. Albeit, they are mostly about automation, efficient office layout, customising the stock software we use; there are still some entertaining ones.

Outside the office I entertain myself with cycling, gaming, kayaking, quizzes, reading, and watching Youtube videos both the trivial and educational. Some great channels I’ve learned a lot from: Crash Course, MinuteEarth, MinutePhysics, NativLang, Sexplanations and more…

I’ve learned some German, Norwegian, Spanish and improved my Hebrew using my most used app after Whatsapp; Duolingo. I had to break my streak for this application but that’s alright because getting into F&C and doing well is very important to me as it’s where I want to be going forward...
